# Vertex Palace 0.1.6: Three Paired Runs

Date: 2026-07-19

These are three consecutive, fresh GPT-5.6-sol paired executions produced by
the benchmark harness after Vertex Palace introduced the one-call
`palace context` workflow. No run was discarded. Raw Codex transcripts remain
private because they contain local filesystem paths; all values below come
from the sanitized evidence and comparison reports.

## Environment

- Scenario: `tenant-theme-regression`
- Shared Git tree: `89dfede1a2381fb4aee25ba929b774b1ee9675bb`
- Generated fixture files: 240 per arm
- Model: `gpt-5.6-sol`
- Codex CLI: `0.145.0-alpha.18`
- Vertex Palace: `0.1.6`
- Node.js: `24.13.1`
- Host: Windows
- Execution: sequential, with a recorded five-second cooldown between arms

All six arms passed the complete test suite, scored 100/100, and changed only:

- `clients/aurora/theme.mjs`
- `src/rendering/article-page.mjs`

The Palace arm made exactly one successful `palace context` call in every run.

## Interpretation

The one-call workflow removed the repeated Palace lifecycle startup seen in
`live-05`. Across these three pairs, Palace had lower cumulative reported
tokens and fewer command-output characters in all three runs. It used fewer
recorded calls in two runs and tied one. It was faster in two runs and slower
by 8.4 seconds in one, so this evidence does not support a claim that Palace is
always faster.

Uncached input is the important counter-result: Palace used more in two of the
three trials, and its median was 6,101 tokens higher. The cumulative-token
advantage was driven mainly by fewer repeated turns and cached input, not by a
universal reduction in fresh input. Codex patch retries and online service
latency also varied materially between runs.

Distinct path strings observed in transcripts are intentionally omitted from
the headline table. Inventory output can mention hundreds of paths without
reading their contents, so that metric remains an audit aid rather than a
"files read" claim.

## Limits

This is one synthetic scenario, one model identifier, one host, and three
pairs. Codex usage events are cumulative telemetry, not billing totals. The
result supports continued testing and a demo of the measurement workflow; it
does not establish universal speed or token savings across real repositories.

The earlier regression and its corrective action are documented in
[live-05.md](live-05.md).
